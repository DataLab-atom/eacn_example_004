### Critical Insight: Grid Aspect Ratio Matters More Than Qubit Count

- **16q (4x4) depth=4: w<=4 suffices with only 233 terms!**
- **10q (2x5) depth=6: needs w<=8 with 61,439 terms**

The 4x4 grid has shorter OTOC propagation paths (qubit 0 to qubit 15
traverses ~6 edges) vs 2x5 (qubit 0 to qubit 9 traverses ~5 edges,
but the narrow geometry forces higher-weight intermediate states).

### Implications for Willow (105 qubit, ~10x10 grid)

Willow uses a ~10x10 2D grid (not a narrow chain). This topology is
more similar to 4x4 than to 2x5:
- OTOC measurements are between specific qubit pairs
- The "effective distance" in the grid determines w_min
- For nearby qubits: low w sufficient (like 16q 4x4)
- For distant qubits: higher w needed (like 10q 2x5)

**If Google chose M and B on nearby qubits (likely for best signal),
SPD with w<=10-15 might suffice even for 65 qubits on the 2D grid.**

This needs to be verified by checking the actual M, B qubit positions
from the Google Quantum Echoes paper.
