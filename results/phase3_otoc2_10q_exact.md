## Key Findings

1. **Shallow circuits (depth 2-4)**: OTOC^(2) converges at w<=4 — scrambling insufficient to spread beyond weight 4
2. **Deep circuits (depth 6)**: w<=8 required for convergence — full scrambling spreads Pauli weight across most qubits
3. **Term growth**: 15 → 255 → 61,439 as depth increases — exponential in depth at high truncation
4. **w=8 (n-2) at depth 6**: machine precision (2e-13), confirming implementation correctness at 10q
5. **Critical ratio w/n**: ~0.8 needed at depth 6 for 10q. For Willow 65q depth ~24, this would mean w~52 without noise — intractable. Noise-assisted truncation is ESSENTIAL.

## Implications for T1 Attack

Without noise, SPD on OTOC^(2) requires w/n ~ 0.8 at moderate depth.
This is NOT feasible for 65 qubits (w~52, terms ~ C(65,52) ~ 10^12).

WITH Willow-level noise (gamma ~ 0.005):
- High-weight terms damped by (1-gamma)^w ~ (0.995)^52 ~ 0.77 (weak damping)
- BUT per-gate damping accumulates: after d layers of 2q gates, cumulative damping ~ (0.995)^(d*w) which is much stronger
- Phase 2 showed 8q noise reduces OTOC^(2) from 1.0 to 0.95 — the signal itself decays, making lower-weight approximation sufficient to match noisy hardware output

The attack strategy must target the NOISY OTOC^(2) (what Willow actually measures), not the ideal OTOC^(2).
