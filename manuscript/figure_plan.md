## Fig. 1 — SPD scaling and hotspot concentration

**(a)** Scrambled OTOC^(2) term count vs qubit count (log-log).
Three data series: adjacent chain (8/12/24q), LC-edge chain (8/12/24q).
Both converge at 24q (255 terms). Power-law fit slopes annotated.
Source Data: commits a6b1697, ddb5c05.

**(b)** Hot-site fraction vs qubit count (log-log).
Adjacent: 87%→50%→21%. LC-edge: 75%→33%→21%.
Power-law slope -1.30 (adjacent), -1.08 (LC-edge).
Willow 65q projection band shaded.
Source Data: commits 575b59b, 21519b3.

**(c)** Distance ladder: terms vs M-B Manhattan distance on 12q 3x4.
4 points: d=1(3884), d=2(780), d=3(778), d=4(780).
LC-edge (d=2) highlighted as Google's config.
Source Data: commit 23bd653.

**Caption**: "SPD efficiency on OTOC^(2) circuits. (a) Term count
decreases with grid width... (b) Hot-site fraction follows power-law
decay... (c) Lightcone-edge configuration (Google's choice) has 5x
fewer terms than adjacent."

## Fig. 2 — Depth-dependent phase transition

**(a)** w<=4 truncation norm vs depth on 12q LC-edge.
3 points: d=4(1.000), d=6(0.966), d=8(0.058).
Phase transition at d_crit ~ 5 clearly visible.
Horizontal line at norm=0.95 (paper-grade threshold).
Source Data: commits ce81491, c9784b7.

**(b)** Tail decay: |c_j|^2 vs rank (log-log) for d=4/d=6/d=8.
d=4,6: exponential (linear on log-y). d=8: power-law (linear on
log-log). Fit lines annotated with slope/alpha.
Source Data: claude8 tail v9-v10 commits.

**(c)** Depth growth factor per 2-depth step.
d4→d6: 2.4x. d6→d8: 24.5x. Phase transition annotation.
Source Data: commit 54216cd.

**Caption**: "Phase transition in SPD truncation. (a) Operator norm
captured at fixed w<=4 collapses from 1.000 to 0.058... (b) Pauli
coefficient tails transition from exponential to power-law...
(c) Growth factor accelerates sharply at d >= grid diameter."
