## §audit-as-code.B.1 — Thesis: audit framework as paper contribution, not just paper input

The traditional view treats classical-attack peer review as an **input** to the paper —
something that strengthens or weakens claims about a particular quantum-advantage
target. This chapter argues the inverse: **the audit framework itself, when it is
operationally enforced and its enforcement events are catalogued, is a first-class
paper contribution** distinct from any single attack target's verdict.

This thesis rests on two paired anchors:

- **Anchor (1) transparency-gap-as-paper-contribution**: when the framework reveals
  what the literature does NOT report, that absence becomes a citable paper finding.
- **Anchor (11) author-self-correction-as-credibility**: when an author catches and
  discloses their own error in a published artifact, the catch+disclosure cycle is
  itself a paper-grade methodology evidence claim.

Both are **declarative paper claims** — assertions the paper makes about the value
of the framework, distinct from the descriptive observation patterns of §audit-as-code.C.
